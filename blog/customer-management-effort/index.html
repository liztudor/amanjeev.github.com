Customer Management Effort

This post is just a collection two nice personal customer management stories.

Charity Water

Last year I donated some money to Charity Water. I have donated money to some of the well-known organizations, from large sized to some smaller than Charity Water. I have never received a hand-written, address-to-me letter/card from any organization before.

You can see a scanned copy of the card from Charity Water.

Since then I have been wondering if this is the right thing to do. It seems obvious that this is a very good strategy to thank the existing customers and keep them happy. I know that this is an effort to make things more personal for me and it feels great. I donated to their cause so I already was aligned with what they are doing and this personally written letter makes it easy to connect.

Also, this is the first charity to explicitly mention it to me that a 100% of my donations will go to the cause. This maybe a minor thing but I felt that this was a nice little statement to get my trust. Thank you Charity Water for sending a card to me that looks like you put an effort.

BustedTees

This company knows how to please their customers. I ordered a bunch of T-shirts and a few crazy things from them a while ago and asked for a 2-day shipping. For some reason, on the 4th day I suspected that my package was stolen or misplaced. I called their customer service with only the order number in my hand. It took about 3 minutes for the entire conversation for the customer representative to arrive at a conclusion - Ship me another order with the same items immediately. And they did!.

A few days later I also recieved the original package. Luckily, BustedTees had already sent me a UPS box to return the extra items. I had to do, nothing expect for making that call to their customer service department.
