Photography and Charity

I recently came across this excellent photographer Moyan Brenn [Flickr, Twitter], who happens to have a charity campaign. On his charity web page where he urges everyone to join. He says -

Every month i am personally sending 20 dollars to a little boy in Africa……with my photo gallery i’m trying to involve people in donating money to the third world….it’s my main objective and why i want my pictures to be spread over the world. I have something beautiful to communicate which lies behind the strong colors of my pictures, and want it to be useful for those suffering in the third world

He goes on to explain further on how you can join the charity campaign -

Anyway, optionally, if you can afford it and you really appreciate my works, I kindly invite you to make a donation, or to help me to diffuse my pictures by linking my blog in your website just if you decide to use my pictures, or, still, by inviting other people to use them for free and to donate as well.

If you do it, by sending me a receipt of the donation by email, your name will be inserted in the acknowledgement list below, and in the acknowledgement list reported in my flickr profile, where i will also include a link to your website or blog if you need it.

If you missed it and have not visited his flickr, you should do that now because he really is a great photographer and I am sure a lot of us can make use of his photographs and skills. I also really liked the idea and model of his charity. He is passionate about photography and charity and he is achieving both here.

I personally have mentioned my charity effort on my 500px page as -

Any purchase of my pictures or any donation for my work will go to the humanitarian causes. I am not affiliated to these organizations and you are free to donate to these yourself. Prajwala [for the victims of sex trafficking]

To be honest, so far I have sold only one photograph (amounts to $2 at this point). Then this morning I realized that maybe people are skeptic of such purchases. Maybe, they think that I am raking in cash and not donating (although looking at my amateur skills, it is evident I ain't selling a lot). I'd be skeptic about this too. Funny that when we donate to a charity, the transparency we demand from the charity is so high. I wish we did this with for-profit companies also. But I digress.

So, I contacted 500px over twitter regarding this. The idea is simple - prove the authenticity of a photographer's charity status to the potential buyers. Here are some key points that I thought of -

  • 500px acts as a mediator between charities/non-profits and photographer's purchases.
  • One can easily connect to and build a list of various non-profits around the globe.
  • Photographers can display how much percentage of their profits from 500px do they want to donate to the charities but this will be displayed on their profile.
  • Since, 500px is the mediator, they can control these donations and award a so called - Charity Badge - to the photographer to be displayed on the profile. This alone will give confidence of the users/buyers to purchase something from someone who donates.
  • Photographers can also list non-profits they donate to on their respective profile to facilitate this further. The more details in the profile, more will be user confidence.

I am unaware of the legalities involved but I don't see how it will be a big deal, except that 500px will have to do a lot of work and being a startup they might have to run the numbers on how beneficial this will be for them. So, maybe we (the Phogrammers or Protographers) can use 500px's API to create a service that lets people achieve this without 500px having to do all the work? I am not sure yet, but I think this is a start in some kind of charity campaign that can get passionate photographers make an effort to sell more and buyers of their work to make more humanitarian choices.
